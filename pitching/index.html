Why reactive enforcement is not enough

Non-compliance usually leaves traces before it becomes an enforcement case.

01

Late or missing filings

Annual Return and Financial Statement lodgement patterns reveal recurring administrative risk.

02

Weak corporate records

Gaps in statutory books, company secretary information, or supporting documents can indicate governance weakness.

03

Entity profile changes

Frequent changes in directors, address, shareholders, or company status become stronger signals when combined with filing history.

04

Repeat-risk behaviour

Prior notices, compounds, complaints, and prosecution history help officers focus on higher-risk entities first.

Risk indicators and AI model design

The model must be accurate, explainable, and useful for officers.

Candidate indicators

  • Annual Return filing status and filing delay
  • Financial Statement lodgement and modified opinion signals
  • Company secretary, director, address, and ownership changes
  • Prior notices, compounds, complaints, prosecution files
  • Dormancy, expired registration, or unresolved charge patterns

Evaluation package

Accuracy Precision Recall F1-score ROC-AUC Confusion matrix

Explainability report identifies the top factors behind each risk score.

Governance, security, and responsible AI

Trust must be designed into the prototype from day one.

PDPA alignment

Use approved data, define processing purpose, and minimise personal data exposure.

Role-based access

Separate research, technical, and enforcement officer access.

Explainable AI

Each risk score includes reason codes and factor contribution.

Bias and quality checks

Validate model behaviour across company categories and risk levels.

Audit log

Record alert generation, officer review, action taken, and case status.

SSM-controlled deployment

Prototype access remains subject to SSM governance requirements.
